This section lists and introduces blogs that I and my peers have published on [AWS Study Group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/awsstudygroupfcj).

### [Blog 1 - DECODING APACHE CELEBORN ON AMAZON EMR](3.1-Blog1/)
This article shares how to use Apache Celeborn as a Remote Shuffle Service (RSS) on Amazon EMR to optimize costs (using EC2 Spot Instances) and improve performance for Big Data processing with Apache Spark.

### [Blog 2 - BUILDING A MULTI-ACCOUNT PATCH MANAGEMENT DASHBOARD WITH KIRO](3.2-Blog2/)
This article shares practical lessons learned from building a fully secure Serverless Dashboard for multi-account AWS Patch Management, applying a Specification-Driven Development workflow combined with the Kiro AI tool.

### [Blog 3 - FROM MULTI-AZ TO SINGLE-AZ – OPTIMIZING COSTS ON AWS](3.3-Blog3/)
This article shares real-world experience optimizing costs for the Team Task Management project on AWS by migrating the architecture from Multi-AZ to Single-AZ, hosting the static frontend on Amazon S3 and CloudFront, and replacing Cron Jobs with AWS Lambda and Amazon EventBridge.

## Outcomes and Knowledge Gained

Through researching, writing, and publishing these three technical blogs, I gained valuable practical knowledge in cloud architecture, operations, and software development on AWS. Specifically, I:

- Developed a deeper understanding of AWS cost optimization strategies and the differences between demo-oriented architectures and production-ready environments.
- Learned how Apache Spark Shuffle works and understood the role of Apache Celeborn in separating compute resources from shuffle storage.
- Gained knowledge of Specification-Driven Development using Kiro and the importance of Steering Files in maintaining project consistency and development standards.
- Improved my awareness of security considerations and best practices when designing and operating internal DevOps systems.
- Enhanced my ability to read, analyze, summarize, and present technical content from AWS Technical Blogs and other cloud-related resources.
- Learned how to connect research findings and architectural concepts to practical implementations, particularly in the Team Task Management project.

These three blogs represent three different areas of learning: practical experience from a real-world project, Big Data architecture research on AWS, and Cloud Operations combined with AI-assisted software development. Together, they significantly expanded my technical perspective and strengthened my readiness for working in professional cloud computing environments.
